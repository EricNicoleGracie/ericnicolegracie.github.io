How We Met
We met on a dating app called OkCupid. Here is a screen shot of our first messages. It is very embarrassing.



We were both attending Cal Poly at the time, Eric finishing his Bachelors degree in Mechanical Engineering and Nicole at the end of her second year in Computer Science. We first met in person at the Campus Market Starbucks on campus, where we sat outside, drank iced black coffees, chatted about our spring breaks, and got sunburned (at least Eric did). Our first few dates were going to SLO farmer’s market, hiking the Cal Poly architecture graveyard, watching Wayne’s World at the Palm Theatre, and watching The Life Aquatic in Eric’s living room.

How We Got Engaged
Eric, Nicole, and Gracie were walking through Seward park in Seattle. We stopped at the edge of Lake Washington to skip rocks, just like we used to at Laguna Lake in San Luis Obispo. Eric is great at skipping rocks, but Nicole basically just throws the rocks in the water as hard as she can. After Nicole had given up on skipping rocks, Eric begged her to try one more time. Nicole started searching for a flat rock on the ground, and when she turned around, Eric was on one knee. He said, “Nicole Giusti, will you marry me?” Nicole hastily shoved the ring on her finger before saying “sure, I guess”. While walking away from the lake, Nicole immediately slipped and fell on a slippery log. After finishing our walk through Seward park, we got brunch with Gracie to celebrate.
